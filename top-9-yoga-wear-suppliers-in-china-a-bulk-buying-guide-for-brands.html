Reading the Table Without Getting It Wrong

Three things worth flagging before you start firing off inquiries.

Capacity numbers are benchmarks, not audited figures. Some factories don't publish verified production data. For those, estimates come from regional cluster norms and comparable Alibaba-tier OEMs. Use them for relative comparison. Jinlong at 800K+ pcs versus Doven at 80–120K tells you something real about production priority and scheduling flexibility. Take the exact numbers as directional guides, not hard facts.

Compliance certificates exist at two levels — factory level and fabric level. Those are different things. Several activewear suppliers here hold OEKO-TEX certification through their mill partners, not at the garment factory itself. That gap matters if your retail buyer needs factory-level audit documentation. Always ask: "Is this your factory's certificate or your fabric supplier's?"

Price ranges assume standard yoga sets at mid-spec. Recycled yarn, GRS certification, bonded seams, or proprietary wicking finishes each push costs up. Build a 10–20% buffer into any FOB estimate from this table before you run it through a landed cost model.

Conclusion

You've done the research. Now act on it.

Brands that scale and brands that stall don't split on product quality or marketing budget. The real gap is supply chain clarity. After reviewing these nine manufacturers, three things stand out:

  • MOQ flexibility matters more at launch than price

  • Seamless yoga wear production is now a baseline requirement for any serious factory

  • The right private label yoga clothing partner for a startup looks nothing like the right one for a $5M brand

Keep your shortlist tight — no more than three suppliers. Send your tech pack and a direct RFQ to each one. Do it in the same 48-hour window. Running those conversations side by side shows you fast who replies with real answers and who pastes a generic quote.
