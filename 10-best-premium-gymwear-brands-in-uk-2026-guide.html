1. Berun Activewear — Best UK-Founded Luxury Gymwear Brand

Berun Activewear is doing what most UK-founded brands only talk about. It builds luxury gymwear designed here , for the way women here live and move.

American giants like Lululemon and Alo Yoga tend to lead the premium activewear space. Berun takes a different path. It's a design-led British label built around real UK life — early morning studio sessions, post-gym commutes, reformer Pilates on a Tuesday lunch break.

What sets Berun apart from the imports?

  • Aesthetic sensibility : Think muted, minimalist colourways — black, charcoal, taupe, olive. Clean silhouettes layer well under a coat. They're not just built for a gym mirror.

  • Fabric integrity : Leggings meet a 250–280 g/m² 4-way stretch technical knit standard. They're squat-proof, non-sheer, with compression that holds without restricting.

  • Sizing that means it : You get a UK 4–22 range with consistent grading and honest size charts. Good fit isn't an afterthought here.

  • Wash durability : These pieces survive at least 30 machine washes at 30°C with less than 10% elasticity loss. That's the standard Berun builds to.

Price range : Leggings £60–£90 · Sports bras £35–£60 · Co-ordinated sets £95–£150

Berun also works as a fitness apparel factory for growing brands.

The activewear market is on track to hit $720 billion by 2034 . Berun's position is clear — a British brand that refuses to cut corners on quality.

2. Lululemon UK — Best Premium Leggings & All-Round Performance Gymwear

Lululemon didn't build its reputation by accident — it built it fabric by fabric.

ELLE and performance experts have tested Lululemon and ranked it among the best premium leggings brands in the UK. The reason isn't branding. It's engineering. Each legging in the range is built around a specific activity. The fabric is the decision.

Four fabrics worth knowing:

  • Nulu — Buttery-soft, second-skin feel. Built for yoga, barre, and low-impact studio work. You get maximum comfort, but very little abrasion resistance. Skip this one for HIIT.

  • Luon — Stretch-cotton feel with ~87% nylon, 13% Lycra elastane. A solid studio fabric for Pilates and light training.

  • Everlux — Lululemon's fastest-drying fabric. It has two layers: a cool interior and a smooth exterior. The go-to for HIIT, CrossFit, and spin. Best all-round pick for mixed training.

  • Luxtreme — The most intense performance fabric in the range. Firm compression, sleek feel, and three thickness levels to choose from. Built for running and endurance.

Price range : £88–£128 for full-length performance styles.

The cost-per-wear numbers hold up. A £108 Everlux legging used three times a week for 18 months comes to £0.46 per wear . That's strong value against cheaper options that lose compression after 100–120 washes.

Bottom line : Heavy trainers who mix disciplines — Everlux is your default . Many startups benchmark these fabrics before selecting an activewear OEM services partner.Dedicated runners should go Luxtreme . Pure studio training? Nulu is the one to look at.

Alo started in Los Angeles in 2007. Today, it's a $10 billion brand pulling in close to £1.6 billion in annual revenue. The UK was its first European market, with stores on Regent Street, in Knightsbridge, and in Covent Garden. Those locations weren't chosen by chance. They put Alo right in front of the luxury wellness shopper.

What makes Alo worth the price?

  • Airbrush Leggings : High-waist, matte finish, zero front seam. Real yogis put these through Reformer flows, inversions, and deep stretches — they stay put every time

  • Fabric feel : The nylon-elastane build feels like a second skin. You get softness without that slippery, clinical texture other leggings have

  • Studio-to-street design : Neutral colourways take you from the mat to Marylebone. Nothing about them reads as pure gymwear

Price range : Leggings £80–£130

Luxury positioning often comes before gym clothing wholesale price considerations.

Alo sells a lifestyle, not just a legging. UK Pilates and yoga communities are buying into that idea more and more. For those who do, the quality makes the spend easy to justify.

4. Adanola — Best Minimalist Athleisure Brand for Everyday Luxury

Manchester, 2015. Two brothers, a modest budget, and months of revenue that cleared just £10k. Nobody would have bet on it then.

Fast-forward to 2025: £112M in sales, a £400M valuation, and a single legging — the Ultimate Legging — that has sold 1.5 million pairs . That's not a fluke. It's one of British fashion's most compelling growth stories.

The brand's aesthetic is precise and deliberate. Black, oat, olive, charcoal. Clean high-waist silhouettes. The famous ADA-logo hoodie is now a fixture across TikTok and Pinterest. Nothing about it shouts — it just looks right , everywhere.

Where Adanola excels:

  • Gym-to-coffee versatility : Pair leggings with an oversized hoodie. You go straight from a Pilates class to a Saturday brunch — no outfit change needed

  • All-day comfort : Light-to-medium compression puts wearability first. It's built for long days, not just workouts

  • Accessible price point : Leggings sit at £40–£60 — below Lululemon's entry line

Worth knowing : Adanola leans lifestyle over performance. For high-intensity training or long-distance running, Lululemon's specialised fabrics still have the edge. But for the woman who wants one clean, versatile wardrobe that moves with her day? Adanola earns its place.

Price range : Leggings £40–£60 · Sets from £80.Its clean aesthetic also appeals to many activewear wholesalers.

Christian Guzman founded it as a bodybuilding creator. It started as a tight community of physique-driven athletes. It grew into a full premium brand. The focus is on clothing that supports serious training — not a fashion label dressed up in gym kit.

Built for how lifters move:

Alphalete fills a distinct lane. It's not a multi-brand platform like Urban Gym Wear. It's not a lifestyle-first label either. 9. Fabletics UK — Best Value-Premium Gymwear for Frequent Wardrobe Refresh

Fabletics UK runs on one simple idea: you shouldn't pay Lululemon prices every time you want a new gym kit. The whole model is built around making regular wardrobe updates affordable.

The VIP membership is the core of it. Pay £54.99–£59.99 per month , and you get one credit. That credit covers any single item, outfit, or bundle worth up to £80 . Most complete sets — leggings plus sports bra — come to £59.99 for VIP members . Non-members pay closer to £80–£100 for the same sets. That's a steady 25–40% saving on every purchase.

The opt-out option is just as important as the discount itself. Hit "Skip the Month" between the 1st and 5th , and nothing gets charged. Credits stay valid for 12 months , so shoppers who buy less often can stack three credits and spend them all in one go.

Who this works for:

  • Gym-goers who need at least one new piece per month

  • Anyone planning a seasonal wardrobe overhaul of three to four sets

  • Shoppers who stay on top of their subscription and skip months they won't buy

Who it doesn't work for : BBC reported users racking up close to £5,000 in charges over seven years with no equivalent purchases to show for it. Miss a few skips, and the costs add up fast.

On quality, Fabletics sits above fast fashion, below Lululemon . You get high-performance fabrics, solid compression, and squat-proof construction across both men's and women's lines. It won't beat Lululemon's Everlux or Nulu on technical innovation. But for a wardrobe that looks sharp and holds up through several sessions a week, it earns its spot without question.

Price range : VIP sets from £59.99 · Non-member leggings £60–£90 · Free delivery on orders over £49.95

How to Choose Premium Gymwear in the UK: Buyer's Checklist (2026)

Spending £80 on gymwear and getting it wrong is a specific kind of frustration. Here's how to stop that happening.

Match the Fabric to Your Training Style

The fabric that works for Reformer Pilates will let you down under a barbell. Know what you're buying before you commit.

Strength training — Look for polyester/nylon blends of at least 70%, with 15–25% elastane. You want a mid-to-high waist that stays put under load. Get reinforced fabric at the glutes and front thighs. Those are the spots where equipment contact wears the fabric out fastest.

Yoga & Pilates — Prioritise softness and four-way stretch. Nylon/polyester (65–75%) with higher elastane (20–30%) gives that "second-skin" feel. Wide waistbands of at least 8–10cm help too. No hard stitching means you stay comfortable through long holds.

Running & HIIT — Moisture-wicking, quick-dry fabrics are non-negotiable. Mid-to-high compression (20–30% elastane) cuts muscle oscillation during runs. Mesh panels at the back of the knee and sides manage heat build-up. A zip pocket at the rear waistband keeps your phone stable through every interval.

Three Tests Before You Buy

Squat-proof check : Stretch the fabric between your fingers. Hold it up to the light. Fabric that goes translucent at 120% stretch will go see-through in a deep squat. Quality leggings hold their colour and density at full extension.

Compression fit : The waistband should allow one to two fingers' width. Stand, take a deep breath, then jump ten times. A drop of more than 2–3cm at the waist means it won't hold during HIIT.

Wash durability : High-end fabrics survive 30+ machine washes at 30°C with no real elasticity loss. Check the care label. Brands serious about longevity build that standard into their spec from the start.

Sustainability: Separating Real Credentials from Marketing Noise

Look for GOTS , OEKO-TEX STANDARD 100 , or GRS certification — with a certificate number, not just a logo. Recycled content needs to be stated in plain numbers: "recycled polyester 68%" means something real. "Eco-conscious" with no percentages means nothing.

Brands with genuine credentials publish supply chain data, factory lists, and annual sustainability reports. No specifics on the website? Treat the claim with scepticism.

UK-Specific Buying Practicalities

  • Returns window : Most premium brands offer 30 days. Check whether sealed hygiene tags apply to leggings. Breaking the seal on some brands wipes out your return rights in full.

  • Sizing : International brands (Lululemon, Alo, On) use US sizing. Cross-reference your waist and hip measurements in centimetres against the brand's own chart. Don't guess from S/M/L alone.

  • Delivery and VAT : UK-registered brands ship from domestic warehouses. That means 1–3 working days and no customs issues. International brands shipping from EU stock can add 3–7 days, plus surprise import costs at the door.

Conclusion

Your perfect gymwear isn't the one with the biggest logo. It's the one built for your body, your training, and your values.

Take Berun Activewear for sculpted compression. Go with Lululemon for cult-classic comfort. Pick P.E Nation for bold energy. The UK's premium fitness apparel scene in 2026 is packed with strong choices — and that's a good thing. These brands don't just sell gym clothes. They sell confidence, performance, and the quiet satisfaction of getting dressed right .

Stop overthinking it. Bookmark the comparison table. Check your non-negotiables from the buyer's checklist. Then pick the brand that excites you most.
