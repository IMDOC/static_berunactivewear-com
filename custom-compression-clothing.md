## 板块 5: Pressure Measurement & Batch QC [protocol panel + zone target-pressure table + tolerance gauge]
**H2**: How We Verify mmHg — the Compression Clothing Manufacturer's Pressure QC
**Sub**: A compression claim is only as good as the number behind it. We measure target pressure, hold it to ±3 mmHg batch to batch, and read retention after washing — so you can put the mmHg on your hangtag.
**内容**:
- **Measurement method**: an on-leg pressure-mapping rig reads mmHg at defined zones — ankle, calf, thigh — against the graduated profile you approved, not against yarn tension or GSM alone.
- **Target-pressure spec**: a zone target-pressure table (zone × target mmHg × graduated %) is confirmed on your approved sample before bulk runs.
- **Per-lot verification**: every production lot is sampled and read on the rig. The batch-to-batch pressure tolerance is **±3 mmHg** — "light" on one PO does not come back firm on the next.
- **Retention**: target pressure is re-read after **30 home washes** and held within tolerance, so the recovery sleeve that read 18 mmHg new still reads in range after a season of washing.
- **Why it matters**: knitting to GSM and "feel" is how a factory ships a sample that reads firm and a bulk run that reads soft. Measuring to a target — and verifying it per lot — is what lets you label and defend a real mmHg as a mmHg compression apparel supplier should.
- **Compliance reminder**: this is athletic and recovery compression measured as sportswear; it is not a clinically-classified medical-grade compression device.

We knit gradient circular-knit tights and sleeves that step pressure down distal to proximal, and we panel cut-and-sew tops and shorts with firmer zones over the calf, quad, and core. 2. **Can you hit and verify a specific mmHg?** Yes. We measure target pressure on an on-leg rig against your approved profile, confirm a zone target-pressure spec on the sample, and verify every production lot. Batch-to-batch tolerance is ±3 mmHg, and we re-read pressure after 30 washes. That is what lets you label a real mmHg and defend it.
3. **Do you make medical-grade or therapeutic compression?** No. We manufacture athletic and recovery graduated compression — roughly 8–25 mmHg — as sportswear, built to your target mmHg. We do not produce clinically-classified therapeutic or medical-grade compression devices, and we do not carry medical certification. That regulatory classification is the brand's responsibility, and we are glad to build to your stated athletic spec.
4. **Does MOQ 300 apply to a graduated tight the same as a sleeve?** Yes. MOQ is 300 pieces per style, size-split across your size range as you choose, not 300 per size. A gradient-knit graduated tight carries the same 300 minimum as a simpler sleeve — the graduated knit program does not raise the minimum.
5. **How fast is a sample, and what does it cost?** A sample is 1 piece at USD 45, ready in 5 working days when the fabric is in stock, or 12 working days for to-order fabric. You approve the pressure profile, fit, fabric, and color on the sample — including the measured mmHg — before any bulk is cut.

Tops, tights, shorts, and calf and arm sleeves all run on our own lines to one pressure standard and one dye batch, so a coordinated wholesale compression clothing kit reads the same mmHg across pieces and matches in color. The sleeve's 20 mmHg matches the tight's.
7. **What is the private label minimum for compression wear?** Private label runs on our existing compression blanks across the three tiers at a lower entry than full OEM, with your woven label, hangtag, and packaging. It is the fastest route to a first PO; send us your label kit and target pressure tiers to confirm.
8. **How consistent is pressure across reorders?** We hold the target mmHg to ±3 mmHg per lot and read it on the rig in your pre-shipment package, so a reorder reads the same pressure as your first run. As a recovery compression clothing manufacturer, we keep the number stable season to season, not just on the first sample.
