QC Culture

How a 2020 Batch Failure Built Our QC Lab

The home page lists the tolerance specs. This is the story behind them.

The trigger

In late 2020, a fabric supplier shipped two consecutive lots with a 12% GSM variance. Our floor-side spot-checks missed it. The finished batch reached the client, who flagged the weight difference against the previous season’s run. We ate the cost, replaced the order, and lost 6 weeks of line time.

What changed

Y.Z., then a senior inspector, proposed replacing floor-side spot-checks with a dedicated QC lab. By mid-2021, the lab was operational. Every incoming fabric lot now passes through colorfastness testing (wash, rub, light), burst-strength measurement, pilling resistance grading (Martindale), and dimensional stability checks — before a single meter reaches the cutting room.

The team behind it

Today Y.Z. leads 32 QC inspectors and 8 lab technicians. The inspectors run in-line and final inspection across all 12 production lines. The lab technicians test every incoming fabric lot against your approved swatch — color at ΔE ≤ 1.5, weight at GSM ± 5%, sizing at ± 0.5 cm. Those tolerances are published on our home page for anyone to verify.

The rework/refund commitment. If a batch exceeds tolerance after your QC or a third-party audit flags it, we rework or refund. No negotiation, no finger-pointing. That is a cultural decision L.C. made in 2020 — not a policy added to a contract template. It is why our 12-month AQL 2.5 pass rate sits at 98.5%.
