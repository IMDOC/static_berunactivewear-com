Roster Workflow

From Spreadsheet to Finished Kit in Four Steps

1

Upload CSV

Fill three columns: player name, number, size. One row per piece. Save as .csv and attach to your inquiry.

2

Data Validation

Pre-press checks for duplicate numbers, character-length overflows, and missing sizes by the next working day. A marked-up proof sheet is returned if corrections are needed.

3

Per-Piece Sublimation

Each piece runs through the sublimation line with its own merged file — number, name, crest, and sponsor block composited from your validated roster.

4

QC Cross-Check

Every finished piece is scanned against the original roster row: correct name spelling, correct number, correct size label. Mismatches trigger reprint before packing.

Pre-Production Checklist

What We Need Before Production Starts

  • Roster CSV

    .csv format, UTF-8 encoding, columns: name | number | size. A downloadable template is available on the contact page.

  • Design Files

    Team crest in vector (AI/EPS/SVG), sponsor artwork as a separate layer, jersey template in PDF or AI with bleed marks.

  • Size Chart Confirmation

    Finalized size run (XS–3XL distribution) locked before sample approval. Changes after approval reset the 18–25 day production clock.

  • Sample Approval Cutoff

    Written approval required via email before bulk production begins. Unapproved samples hold the queue.

DEADLINE ARITHMETIC

Work backwards from the first fixture, not forwards from today

Season orders fail on calendars more than on garments. Run this subtraction before you promise the club anything.

1

Fix the immovable date

First match, tournament check-in, or team photo day — whichever comes first is the real deadline, and it is usually earlier than the season opener everyone quotes.

2

Subtract transit and customs

Your freight lane sets this number, not us. Express, air and sea differ by weeks; pick the lane at order time so the production slot is booked against a real arrival date.

3

Reserve the production window

The 18–25 days start at validated roster plus approved proof — not at first contact. Budget your own committee’s approval speed honestly; it is the step factories cannot accelerate.

4

What remains is roster time

The date this arithmetic produces is the day your spreadsheet must be final. Publish it to players and parents early — chasing sizes is always the slowest link.

ROSTER REALITY

Squads change. The order should expect it.

  • Mid-Season Signings

    Agree the top-up mechanism when ordering: spare blank kits held for transfer windows, or a small follow-up run. Deciding in advance turns a crisis into a line item.

  • Keeper & Alternate Variants

    Goalkeeper colorways, captain marks and clash kits live as extra columns in the same CSV, not separate orders — one file keeps numbering and crests consistent across variants.

  • Multi-Squad Files

    Clubs running first team, reserves and juniors submit one sheet per squad against shared artwork. Deliveries can stagger by squad while the visual identity stays locked.

  • Name Changes & Corrections

    Spelling fixes are free during validation and expensive after sublimation. The proof sheet we return is the last cheap moment — have a parent check every junior’s name on it.
