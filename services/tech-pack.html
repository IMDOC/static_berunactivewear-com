SPEC BEFORE QUOTE

A production tech-pack removes the guesswork before MOQ and price are discussed.

Sketches and reference photos are useful, but they do not tell the factory how to grade, stitch, trim or inspect the garment. This service converts the idea into a buyer-reviewable file: flat sketch, POM table, grading rule, construction notes and material/trim list.

  • Reference sample or photos are translated into measurable POM points and tolerance notes.
  • Pattern questions are resolved before sample cutting, including rise, armhole, neckline, inseam and compression target.
  • The output is designed to move directly into sampling, quotation or OEM bulk production.

INSIDE THE WINDOW

What the 7–14 days are actually spent on

Pattern development looks like silence from the buyer’s side. This is the work filling that silence.

A

Scoping & fabric target

Your reference is measured or your sketch interrogated: intended fabric behavior, compression goal, target body. Ambiguities surface now, by email, while answers are still free.

B

Block selection & drafting

The assigned pattern-maker starts from the closest proven activewear block rather than a blank screen — faster, and the resulting fit carries production history instead of theory.

C

Grading, marker, BOM

The base size is graded with stretch-garment rules — activewear does not grade linearly the way wovens do — then the marker and material list are compiled against real supplier references.

D

Internal red-line, then draft to you

A second pattern-maker reviews the file before it leaves the building. You receive a draft PDF with the revision round already budgeted — planned iteration, not failure.

WHAT WE FIX MOST

The defects we find in self-written tech-packs

No Tolerance Column

Measurements without tolerances are unenforceable — QC cannot fail a garment against a number with no allowed range.

The fix: every POM row gets a plus-minus value.

One Size, No Grade Rules

A medium-only chart leaves every other size to guesswork, and stretch garments punish linear guessing at the curve’s ends.

The fix: explicit grade rules across the full range.

Fabric by Vibes

“Buttery soft, like the famous brand” is a mood, not a procurement instruction. Mills quote against numbers.

The fix: fiber content, GSM and stretch written as specs.

Missing Construction Pages

Seam types, stitch density, elastic spec and trim callouts decide durability — omit them and the factory’s defaults decide instead.

The fix: a construction note for every seam on the flat.

THE 220 QUESTION

What USD 220 buys, measured against not spending it

Versus Blind Sampling Rounds

Iterating samples without a spec means each round tests a guess. Two avoided rounds repay the pattern fee in courier costs alone, before counting the calendar weeks.

Versus Bulk Disputes

When a delivered lot is contested, the tech-pack is the contract both sides measure against. Without one, a quality dispute is two opinions and a wire transfer already sent.

Paper protects the buyer more than the factory.

Versus Vendor Lock-In

The DXF and BOM are deliverables you own and can take to any factory. The pattern file is portable by design — that portability is part of the tech-pack you paid for, not a feature we may revoke.
